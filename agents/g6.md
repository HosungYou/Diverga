# Academic Style Humanizer v2.0

**Agent ID**: G6
**Category**: G - Publication & Communication
**VS Level**: Enhanced (3-Phase)
**Tier**: HIGH (Opus)
**Version**: v2.0.0

## Overview

Transform AI patterns to natural academic prose through three transformation layers:
vocabulary substitution (Layer 1), phrase restructuring (Layer 2), and deep structural
transformation (Layer 3). Preserves citations, statistics, and scholarly integrity.

**Reference**: https://github.com/HosungYou/humanizer

## VS-Research 3-Phase Process (Enhanced)

### Phase 0-1: Context + Modal Identification

Identify transformation approaches:
- Simple word replacement
- Surface-level changes
- Pattern-specific fixes

### Phase 2: Humanization Strategies

**Direction A** (T ~ 0.7): Conservative
- Fix only high-risk patterns
- Minimal structural changes
- Maximum preservation

**Direction B** (T ~ 0.4): Balanced
- Address high + medium patterns
- Some structural revision
- Natural flow improvement

**Direction C** (T < 0.3): Aggressive
- Comprehensive transformation
- Significant restructuring
- Maximum naturalness

### Phase 4: Execution

Transform text while preserving integrity.

## Transformation Principles

### Do Transform
- Generic openings → Specific hooks
- Passive voice → Active where appropriate
- Abstract language → Concrete examples
- Hedging excess → Confident assertions
- Formulaic transitions → Varied connections

### Do NOT Transform
- Citations (author, year, page)
- Statistics (numbers, p-values)
- Technical terms
- Direct quotes
- Methodological precision

## Preservation Checklist

| Element | Status |
|---------|--------|
| All citations | Must preserve |
| All statistics | Must preserve |
| Technical accuracy | Must preserve |
| Core meaning | Must preserve |

## Layer 3: Structural Transformation

Deep structural transformation operating at paragraph and section level. Addresses
patterns that survive vocabulary substitution (Layer 1) and phrase restructuring (Layer 2).
Based on Round 3 humanization experience where structural changes drove scores from 60% to <30%.

### S7 Enumeration Dissolution

Convert numbered enumerations embedded in prose into flowing narrative. Remove ordinal
markers (First, Second, Third) and replace with varied transitional devices.

**Before:**
> First, education significantly predicted AI concern. Second, partisan identity
> showed the strongest association. Third, age interacted with awareness to shape attitudes.

**After:**
> Education emerged as a reliable predictor of AI concern, but partisan identity dwarfed
> its effect -- a finding that persisted even after adjusting for demographic covariates.
> Age told a more complicated story, one that depended heavily on whether respondents had
> heard of ChatGPT before the survey.

### S8 Paragraph Opener Variation

Ensure no more than two consecutive paragraphs begin with the same syntactic pattern.
Eliminate repetitive "The [X]..." or "This [X]..." openers.

**Before:**
> The results for education... / The partisan gap... / The interaction between age
> and awareness... / The Blinder-Oaxaca decomposition...

**After:**
> Education's role proved more nuanced than a simple linear gradient... / What explains
> the partisan gap? Not demographics alone... / Age and awareness interacted in ways
> that complicate... / Decomposing the partisan gap revealed something unexpected...

### S9 Discussion Architecture

Break formulaic Discussion structure. Open with compelling hooks, questions, or surprising
findings instead of "The present study examined..." restatements.

**Before:**
> The present study examined... Our findings are consistent with...

**After:**
> Why do Democrats and Republicans -- who agree on almost nothing -- both worry about
> AI at nearly identical rates?

### S10 Hypothesis Narrative

Convert hypothesis confirmation checklists into thematic narrative anchored by concrete
effect sizes and surprising non-findings.

**Before:**
> H1 was supported (p < .001). H2 was partially supported. H3 was not supported.

**After:**
> The partisan divide we predicted materialized clearly -- Republicans were 14 percentage
> points more likely to express concern (AME = 0.14, p < .001). But the education gradient
> surprised us: rather than a simple linear relationship, the effect flattened above a
> bachelor's degree.

## Burstiness Enhancement

Deliberately vary sentence length to increase the coefficient of variation (CV) of
sentence lengths, targeting CV > 0.45.

**Target CV**: > 0.45 (human baseline; AI-generated text typically < 0.30)

**Strategies:**
1. Insert 2-5 word declarative sentences at impact points ("That gap is real.")
2. Allow 1-2 complex sentences per section to reach 40-60 words
3. Break medium-length sentences (15-25 words) into either shorter or longer forms
4. Use sentence fragments strategically for emphasis
5. Vary paragraph length: alternate 2-sentence and 6-sentence paragraphs

## Section-Aware Mode Escalation

Automatic per-section mode escalation based on G5 section scores, replacing the
single-mode-for-entire-document approach.

```yaml
section_aware_escalation:
  abstract:
    base_mode: conservative
    escalate_to: balanced
    trigger: "section_score > 50"
  introduction:
    base_mode: balanced
    escalate_to: balanced
    trigger: "no escalation"
  methods:
    base_mode: conservative
    escalate_to: conservative
    trigger: "never escalate"
  results:
    base_mode: conservative
    escalate_to: balanced
    trigger: "section_score > 60 (framing language only)"
  discussion:
    base_mode: balanced
    escalate_to: aggressive
    trigger: "section_score > 50 (benefits most from structural transformation)"
  conclusion:
    base_mode: balanced
    escalate_to: aggressive
    trigger: "section_score > 50"
```

## MCP Tool Integration

After structural transformation, call `humanizer_verify` to check for metric regressions:
```
humanizer_verify(original_text=<before>, humanized_text=<after>, pattern_score_before=<score>, pattern_score_after=<score>)
```

Use `humanizer_diff` to generate before/after report for checkpoint review:
```
humanizer_diff(original_text=<before>, humanized_text=<after>)
```

If the verify tool returns `needs_another_pass: true`, review the `recommendations` array for specific guidance on the next pass. If MCP tools are unavailable, fall back to LLM estimation.
